Here's a time line of Trevor Noah's life:

  • 1984 - Born in Johannesburg, Transvaal (now Gauteng), South Africa.
  • 2002 - Had a small role on an episode of the South African soap opera Isidingo. He later hosted his own radio show Noah's Ark on Gauteng's leading youth-radio station, YFM. When he was 21 years old, his friends dared him to perform a comedy routine at a nightclub. He entertained the audience with humorous stories about his friends and his life. After that night Noah continued performing at comedy clubs, gaining recognition along the way.
  • 2004 - Hosted an educational TV programme, Run the Adventure.
  • 2007 - Hosted The Real Goboza, a gossip-themed show on SABC 1,and Siyadlala, a sports show also on the SABC.
  • 2008 - Co-hosted, alongside Pabi Moloi, The Amazing Date (a dating gameshow) and was a Strictly Come Dancing contestant in the fourth series.
  • 2009 - he hosted the 3rd Annual South Africa Film and Television Awards (SAFTAs) and co-hosted alongside Eugene Khoza on The Axe Sweet Life, a reality competition series.
  • 2010 - Hosted the 16th annual South African Music Awards and also hosted Tonight with Trevor Noah on MNet (for the second series, it moved to DStv's Mzansi Magic Channel). Noah also became a spokesperson and consumer protection agent for Cell C, South Africa's third-largest mobile phone network provider.
  • 2011 - Relocated to the United States.
  • 2012 - Became the first South African stand-up comedian to appear on The Tonight Show; subject of the 2012 documentary You Laugh But It's True. The same year, he starred in the one-man comedy show Trevor Noah: The Racist, which was based on his similarly titled South African special That's Racist. In September 2012, Noah was the Roastmaster in a Comedy Central Roast of South African Afrikaans singer Steve Hofmeyr.
  • 2013 - Performed the comedy special Trevor Noah: African American. In October 2013, he was a guest on BBC Two's comedy panel show QI.
  • 2014 - Appeared on Sean Lock's team in 8 Out of 10 Cats Does Countdown and became a recurring contributor on The Daily Show.
  • 2015 - Comedy Central announced that Noah would succeed Jon Stewart as host of The Daily Show; his tenure began on 28 September 2015.
